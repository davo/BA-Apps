What is a toolkit?

A toolkit provides in-house design teams and collaborators with a brief overview of a brand’s look and feel. This ensures greater consistency across product or business communications by establishing a shared framework for all collaborators to work within. A toolkit will often include brand identity, colors, typographic elements, and other graphic devices, but is less robust and less finite than a comprehensive brand guidelines deck — making them more flexible as a result.

Why is less branding more?

As a young brand, spending any large sum on a dense, meticulously crafted set of brand guidelines may actually diminish your willingness to embrace change. At MojoTech, we approach our own brand standards with the same discipline that we advocate to our clients: start with a single big idea—the core truth at the center of your brand—and build out rules incrementally and organically. A good toolkit will provide purposeful gaps for your product, your designers, and your users to work within and respond to, allowing you to remain nimble. And as your product or business evolves, the continuity of your brand and its core values remain intact.
